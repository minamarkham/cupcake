What's New:

Rape crisis centers across America are available to help victims of rape, sexual assault, and child sexual abuse cope with many issues related to their victimization, including depression, anxiety, relationship problems, medical issues, and criminal justice issues. Help is also available for family members and friends of sexual assault victims. Finally, age-appropriate rape prevention and child sexual abuse prevention programs are available to help reduce your family's risk of victimization. For confidential assistance, you can call the Butler County Rape Crisis Program 24 hours a day, 365 days a year at (513)523-4146, 894-7002, or 424-5498. Outside of the Southwestern Ohio area, you can also find help 24 hours a day through the Rape, Abuse, and Incest National Network at 1-800-656-HOPE.

It is our hope that this site will help survivors and their loved ones to reach out, get help, and heal from the trauma of sexual assault.

What's New:

Sexual abuse disrupts the lives of victims and those who care about them. Dallas Area Rape Crisis Center (DARCC) provides survivors and their families and friends the resources they need to reclaim their lives.

DARCC’s trained volunteers and professional staff are there to help those who have survived sexual abuse to navigate the difficult journey of healing from their trauma in a safe and confidential environment.

An important goal for DARCC is to develop programs that help to promote people’s safety and raise awareness of the issues survivors face. Through prevention and educational programs DARCC will provide learning opportunities that will inspire people to adopt more positive ways of treating each other and ultimately bring about the social change necessary to end sexual violence.

Please use the drop down menus to find more detailed information about DARCC. For more information please contact our office at 972-641-RAPE (7273).
